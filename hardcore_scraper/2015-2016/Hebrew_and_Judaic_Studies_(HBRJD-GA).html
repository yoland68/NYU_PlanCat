HBRJD-GA 2676 Jews & Germans in Weimar & Nazi Germany

This course begins with the cataclysmic end of World War I, the feelings of hurt nationalism and revenge, and examines the political, economic, and social changes in German society as well as parallel developments among German Jews. The course focuses on the rise of Nazism, the social insiders and outsiders in Nazi Germany, the persecutions and reactions of Jews within Germany, and the role persecutors and bystanders.

This course explores the interactions of Jews and Germans after World War II, noting their interlocking histories and memories even after the Holocaust. This seminar explores modern responses to the moral and gender implications of two different constructions of human sacrifice/martyrdom which western culture has inherited from antiquity: the Bible and Greek myth and drama.
